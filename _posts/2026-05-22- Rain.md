Rain falls before the door of the wooden house, and from the eaves, drops descend in quiet lines —
the stone step at the entrance slowly darkens, and the sound of rain fills the threshold whole.
From within, a warm light seeps beneath the door, and the rain pauses at the edge of that glow.
In this moment — neither inside nor out — the rain lingers at the entrance, unhurried, still.
The sound of rain outside holds the indoor silence, and beyond the wooden door, the world grows far.
Lean into this soft and sheltered boundary — and let yourself tilt, gently, into sleep.
