Rain settles upon the low clay roof tiles, and from the eaves, drops fall in quiet rows.
The old earthen jars receive the water in silence, as the courtyard slowly deepens into grey.
Beyond the mud wall, pale rain moves like mist, and persimmon leaves tremble with each drop.
At the edge of the wooden porch where she once sat, only the rain lingers, like a memory long kept.
This rain falling on the courtyard is not new — it has known this place since long before us.
Listen to its familiar, unhurried sound, and let your eyes close, gently, into rest.
