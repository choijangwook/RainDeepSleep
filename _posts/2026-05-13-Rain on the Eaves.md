The trees of the park hear the rain first, and the grass quietly deepens into green — along the empty path, the raindrops spread, and the park breathes slowly, in time with the rain.
Beneath the lamplight, falling rain turns bright, and an empty swing sways gently in the wet.
A single raindrop resting on a petal falls without a sound, returning to the earth.
The park asks nothing as it meets the rain — it simply receives what falls, without resist.
Follow that generous and unhurried sound, and let yourself drift, softly, into sleep.
