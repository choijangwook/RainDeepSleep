Upon the golden heads of ripened rice, the autumn rain descends in quiet grace —
each droplet taps the heavy, swollen grain, and the whole field becomes a gentle drum.
The more they ripen, the lower they bow, and the rain meets their humbling with care.
Upon what sun and wind and time have made, the rain lays down its soft, final touch.
Across the fields, the rain speaks endlessly, and the rice grows full without a word.
Follow this rich and unhurried sound — let it bring you into deep, warm sleep.
